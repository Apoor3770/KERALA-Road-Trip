Munnar Hills — Full Day
Friday, Apr 25
Schedule
6:30 AM
Eravikulam National Park
Nilgiri Tahr mountain goat. Stunning high-altitude grasslands. Opens 7 AM. Book tickets online at eravikulam.org — sells out!
Must-do · Book ahead
9:30 AM
Mattupetty Dam + Echo Point
Scenic reservoir surrounded by shola forest. Boating available. Echo Point 2 km ahead — shout and hear yourself back from the hills.
11:00 AM
Tea Garden Viewpoint + KDHP Factory
Walk through endless emerald tea estates. Factory tour shows full leaf-to-cup process. Buy fresh tea directly from source.
1:00 PM
Lunch · Munnar town
SN Restaurant or Crown Bakery. Browse spice shops, banana chips, handmade chocolate stalls.
3:00 PM
Attukal or Lakkam Waterfalls
Tiered waterfalls through tea-covered hillsides. Refreshing and photogenic.
5:30 PM
Top Station sunset · 1700m
Highest point in Munnar. Panoramic views of Tamil Nadu valleys. Misty clouds rolling in at sunset — unforgettable. 34 km from town, leave by 5 PM latest.
Sunset must
8:00 PM
Return hotel · Dinner
Relax after the big hill day.
Highlights
Endangered mountain goat · Echo Point valley resonance · Fresh-picked tea · Waterfall trek · 360° sunset from 1700m

Munnar → Kumarakom Backwaters Direct
Saturday, Apr 26
Schedule
9:30 AM
Checkout Munnar · Final bazaar stroll
Last chance to buy local tea, spices, Munnar chocolates as gifts.
10:00 AM
Depart for Kumarakom · 120 km direct
Via Kottayam. Scenic descent through rubber plantations and rice paddy fields. ~3 hrs.
Cab · ₹1,800–2,800
1:00 PM
Lunch · Kottayam town
Duck curry (tharavu curry) or beef fry with appam — famous Kottayam Syrian Christian speciality. Ask for a local restaurant, not a hotel.
2:30 PM
Arrive Kumarakom · Check-in
Resort or waterfront houseboat. Freshen up, change into light clothes.
4:00 PM
Backwater canal cruise
Narrow village waterways through paddy fields and coconut groves. Spot kingfishers, egrets, otters. Far more authentic than touristy Alleppey routes.
Must-do
6:00 PM
Sunset over Vembanad Lake
Watch fishermen cast nets silhouetted against the orange sky. Perfect photography.
7:30 PM
Dinner · Karimeen (pearl spot fish)
Local delicacy of Kottayam backwaters. Served with rice, coconut curries. Often cooked tableside at waterfront resorts.
Highlights
Hills-to-backwaters transition in one day · Village canal authenticity · Rare birds in natural habitat · Kerala's most famous fish dish

Kumarakom → Varkala Cliff
Sunday, Apr 27
Schedule
8:00 AM
Checkout · Early breakfast at resort
Enjoy final backwater view from the dining area. Pack up.
8:30 AM
Depart for Varkala · 130 km
Coastal highway south through small towns. Plenty of coconut water stalls to stop at. ~3.5 hrs.
Cab · ₹2,500–4,000
12:00 PM
Arrive Varkala · Check-in cliff hotel
Drop luggage. Get a cliff-view room if possible — worth every rupee.
1:00 PM
Lunch at cliff café
Fish, pizza, Kerala curries all available. Eat with the Arabian Sea in view.
3:00 PM
Cliff walk + Papanasam Beach
Walk narrow cliffside promenade. Browse handicrafts, yoga centres. Descend the stairs to the beach — red cliffs tower 30m above.
5:00 PM
Sunset from the cliff edge
Arguably the best sunset view in Kerala. Arabian Sea goes pink and orange. Sit at a cliffside café with a drink and just watch.
Golden hour
7:00 PM
Cliff-edge dinner
Try Café del Mar, Abba Restaurant, or Juice Shack. Fresh seafood, sound of waves below.
Highlights
Kerala's only cliff beach · 30m red sedimentary cliffs · Sacred Papanasam beach · Clifftop café culture · Best sunset on the trip
